Introduction: Why I Wrote This Book

Empowering Design Education with AI
Exploring Creative Intelligence at the intersection of AI, design, and creativity.

I wrote this book to share the curriculum innovation C.J. Yeh and I have built together over two decades, and to open it up so any educator, student, or working designer can use it. The urgency behind it is real: educators, design students, and young designers need to understand where AI-Assisted Design actually stands right now, and how to prepare for a creative workforce that is already living in that reality. This is my attempt to bridge the gap between academia and the creative workforce, not in theory, but with the actual program we built.

What follows is part memoir, part case study, part field guide. The memoir is mine: thirty years as a designer, more than two decades in the classroom, and the moment AI stopped being a curiosity and became the center of how I teach. The case study is the program C.J. Yeh and I built together at FIT, with the data, the awards, and the industry relationships to back it up. The field guide is the part I most want you to steal: a replicable framework, so you don’t have to build yours from scratch.

I’m not writing this because I have it all figured out. I’m writing it because I’ve figured out enough to be useful to the educators, students, and young designers trying to find their footing in this shift, and I’d rather hand that over now than wait until it’s polished into something less honest.
