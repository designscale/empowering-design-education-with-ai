About the Authors

Christie Shin

Professor | Creative Technology & Design

Education

BFA, MS, MFA, Pratt Institute

Biography

Christie Shin is a Professor of Creative Technology & Design at the Fashion Institute of Technology (SUNY) and a design educator whose work explores the intersection of AI, creativity, and digital product design. With more than three decades of experience as an educator, creative consultant, and design director, she focuses on AI-Assisted Design, UX/UI and product design, branding, and creative workflows built around generative and agentic AI.

At FIT, Shin leads the initiatives integrating AI into design education. She introduced AI-Assisted Design courses beginning in 2025 and co-developed the AI-Assisted Design Minor with C.J. Yeh, part of the six-minor Creative Technology & Design program they co-coordinate. Her research centers on empowering design education with AI and on Creative Intelligence, a framework for how designers collaborate with intelligent systems across ideation, storytelling, prototyping, and product development.

Shin is a 2026 Google Higher Ed Faculty AI Fellow, Inaugural Cohort, and a Founding Member of the Figma Educator Advisory Board (2021–present). She was named an ElevenLabs Impact Professor, giving her students early access to voice AI tools for creative work, and holds parallel educator partnerships with FLORA and Reve. In June 2026 she joins Pratt Institute’s graduate school as a Visiting Professor.

She has organized and curated several initiatives advancing AI in creative practice, including The Age of CI (Creative Intelligence) and an AI Hackathon at FIT, bringing together students, educators, and industry partners to explore new creative workflows with AI. She has presented her research, including “Agentic Design: Architecting the Future of Creative Intelligence,” at institutions such as the University at Albany, contributing to the growing discourse on AI-native design education.

She currently serves as Curriculum Committee Chair and Co-Coordinator of the Creative Technology & Design subject area at FIT. Her contributions to scholarship and creative practice have earned her the SUNY Chancellor’s Award for Excellence in Scholarship and Creative Activities (2023–2024) and the Faculty Excellence Award (2017).

In parallel with her academic work, Shin is Partner and Design Director at Cynda Media Lab, where she has led award-winning work for global clients, earning more than 60 national and international design awards; her work is held in the permanent collection of the Poster House Museum. She also co-founded the Design Portfolio Center (2024). Through her teaching, research, and industry collaborations, she continues to explore how Creative Intelligence expands what designers can do.

C.J. Yeh

Professor | Creative Technology & Design

Education

BFA, Pratt Institute
MFA, University of Pennsylvania

Biography

C.J. Yeh is a Professor of Creative Technology & Design at the Fashion Institute of Technology (FIT), where he has taught since 2003 and built a sustained record of delivering high-quality learning experiences through innovative teaching strategies. He is consistently top-rated by his students.

Yeh is a leader in academic program development. He authored the original Creative Technology Minor in 2013 and the UX/UI Master Certification Program, and co-developed the AI-Assisted Design Minor with Christie Shin, part of the six-minor Creative Technology & Design program they co-coordinate. He has co-chaired the department’s curriculum since 2010 and currently serves as Assistant Chair of the Illustration and Interactive Media Department.

To keep his students at the forefront of the industry, Yeh regularly brings distinguished guest speakers into the classroom from companies including Google DeepMind, Amazon, and Microsoft. He serves as faculty advisor for a range of co-curricular initiatives, including the NFL x FIT contest, the AI Hackathon, and the UGG x Ambush Design Sprint. His teaching has earned him the SUNY Chancellor’s Award for Excellence in Teaching and the FIT President’s Award for Faculty Excellence.

Beyond the classroom, Yeh is a globally recognized scholar and practitioner. He is the author of several influential books, including Art in the Digital Age and The Principles of Interaction Design in the Post-Digital Age, both adopted as textbooks in Taiwan, along with Digital Aesthetics. He regularly represents FIT at international conferences, presenting on Agentic Design and Creative Intelligence.

In parallel with his academic work, Yeh is Founder and Executive Creative Director of Cynda Media Lab, which he founded in 2009; Christie Shin joined as Partner in 2013. His design work has earned more than 50 international honors, including recognition from the iF Design Awards, MUSE, and Communication Arts, and his work is held in the permanent collections of the Poster House Museum and the MAXXI Museum Net Art Archives in Rome. Through his teaching, scholarship, and design practice, Yeh continues to shape how the next generation of designers learns to work alongside intelligent systems.
