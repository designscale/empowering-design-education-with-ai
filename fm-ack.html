Acknowledgments

This book exists because so many people said yes before they had any reason to. Thank you for believing in us, and for placing your trust in us from the very beginning.

I have to begin with C.J. Yeh. He is my partner and mentor. Someone who inspires me to become a better version of myself every day. We share the same vision, values, and uncompromising standards for quality. In 2010, C.J. created this visionary curriculum, Creative Technology & Design, which we have co-chaired together since 2020. Outside of FIT, I am also a partner and Design Director at Cynda Media Lab (CML), which C.J. founded in 2012.

Over the years, through countless discussions, collaborations, and curriculum development, we gradually shaped the concept of Creative Intelligence together. That shared vision ultimately became the foundation and guiding philosophy of this book.

FIT, the State University of New York, gave us the room to practice, experiment, and grow as educators alongside so many wonderful colleagues. That freedom is what made everything else in this book possible.

The Google Higher Ed Faculty AI Fellowship, Inaugural North America Cohort, pushed that growth even further for me. Through the program, I researched and developed the Cognitive-Process Assessment (CPA) framework, and we are deeply grateful to the Google for Education team for providing the support and space to pursue this work.

None of this remained theory because so many industry leaders and companies chose to invest in education alongside us. To ElevenLabs, Reve, FLORA, Google, Figma, Adobe, and AIGA: thank you for believing in and supporting this work. And to everyone who showed up for our students, whether in a classroom, at a product demo, a hackathon, or a portfolio review, thank you for your generosity, expertise, and time.

A special thank you to Thor Schaeff, who helped make our collaboration with ElevenLabs possible when I first reached out and is now at Google DeepMind, and to Gabi Leibowitz at ElevenLabs, whose support through the ElevenLabs Impact Professor program has been invaluable. Thank you as well to Melisa Seah and Kyra Mo at Reve, and Weber Wong at FLORA, for helping turn these partnerships into meaningful opportunities for our students.

Thank you to the Friends of Figma community leaders whose work made so much of this possible: Kimberlea Hunt, Jordan Scott, and Kelsey Lettko. We are also grateful to the Figma Education team, Lauren McCann and Miguel Cardona, for the opportunity to serve as a founding member of the Figma Educator Advisory Board and help strengthen the connection between education and industry. Thank you as well to Jenna Brandvold at The One Club for Creativity for your partnership and continued support.

Chapter 4 belongs to the people who generously sat down with us and shared their honest perspectives on where this field is truly headed. Thank you to Randy Hunt, Kavin Kapoor, Karen Lin, Weber Wong, Matt Owens, Eunjoo Kim, Julio Soler, Joey Hunter, Jenna Cao, and Kathleen O’Brien for your insight, experience, and wisdom.

The practice side of this work is what keeps the teaching side honest, and that is only possible because of the extraordinary teams at CML and Design Portfolio Center.

Finally, to our parents, families, and friends: thank you for your unconditional love, encouragement, and support. Thank you for giving us the freedom to become exactly who we are.
