Acknowledgments

This book exists because a lot of people said yes before they had any reason to. Thank you for believing in me, and for trusting me first.

I have to start with C.J. Yeh. He is my life-long partner, the person who motivates me to be a better person every day. C.J. and I share the same vision, values, and sense of quality. We’ve been co-chairing this curriculum since 2012, running CML together since 2013, and thinking out loud about all of this long before either of us called it Creative Intelligence. Half of what’s good in this book is his thinking as much as mine.

FIT, State University of New York, gave me the room to practice and grow as an educator, alongside so many wonderful fellow professors. That room is what made everything else in this book possible.

The Google Higher Ed Faculty AI Fellowship, Inaugural North America Cohort, pushed that growth further. Through the program, I researched and developed the Cognitive-Process Assessment (CPA) framework, and I’m grateful to the Google for Education team for the space to do it.

None of this stayed theory, because real companies chose to invest in education alongside me. ElevenLabs, Reve, FLORA, Figma, Framer, Adobe, and AIGA: thank you for believing in and supporting this work. And to everyone who showed up for our students, in a classroom, a demo, a hackathon, or a portfolio review: thank you for your time.

Thank you especially to Thor Schaeff, who made the ElevenLabs collaboration happen when I first reached out and is at Google DeepMind now, and to Gabi Leibowitz at ElevenLabs, who has supported us tremendously through the ElevenLabs Impact Professor program. Thank you also to Melisa Seah and Kyra Mo at Reve, and Weber Wong at FLORA, for making those partnerships real for our students.

Thank you to the Friends of Figma community leaders whose work made so much of this possible: Kimberlea Hunt, Jordan Scott, and Kelsey Lettko. And to the Figma Education team: Lauren McCann, Miguel Cardona, Alex Fagundez, and Joanna Chen. Thank you to Jenna Brandvold at The One Club for Creativity, for your partnership and support.

Chapter 4 belongs to the people who sat down with me and told me the truth about where this field is actually headed. Thank you to Randy Hunt, Eunju Kim, Karen Lin, Weber Wong, Matt Owens, Julio Soler, Joey Hunter, and Jenna Cao, for your insight, experience, and wisdom.

The practice side of this work is what keeps the teaching side honest, and that only happens because of the teams at Cynda Media Lab and Design Portfolio Center.

And to my parents, family, and friends: thank you for your unconditional love and support, and for giving me the freedom to become exactly who I am.
